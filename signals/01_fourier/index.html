Visualize f(t) → F(ω) in 3D: view along Z-axis to see f(t), view along X-axis to see |F(ω)|

Core Equations
F(ω) = ∫₋∞^∞ f(t) · e^{−jωt} dt     ← Forward Fourier Transform
f(t) = (1/2π) ∫₋∞^∞ F(ω) · e^{jωt} dω  ← Inverse Fourier Transform
e^{−jωt} = cos(ωt) − j·sin(ωt)        ← Euler's formula (rotating phasor)
F(ω) is complex-valued. |F(ω)| = amplitude spectrum (what you see in the frequency domain), ∠F(ω) = phase spectrum.
How to Compute F(ω₀) — the integral step by step
Step 1: Multiply — form the product f(t)·e^{−jω₀t} = f(t)·cos(ω₀t) − j·f(t)·sin(ω₀t)
Step 2: Integrate — Re[F(ω₀)] = ∫f(t)·cos(ω₀t)dt, Im[F(ω₀)] = −∫f(t)·sin(ω₀t)dt
Step 3: Repeat for all ω → builds the full spectrum F(ω)
Geometric view: e^{−jω₀t} is a helix in (Re, Im, t) space. Multiply by f(t) and integrate = find the "center of mass" → that is F(ω₀).
Fourier Series — periodic signals
f(t) = Σₙ cₙ · e^{jnω₀t}    cₙ = (1/T)∫₀ᵀ f(t)·e^{−jnω₀t}dt    ω₀ = 2π/T
Square wave: f(t) = (4/π)·Σ_{k=1,3,5,...} (1/k)·sin(2πkf₀t) — odd harmonics, coefficients ∝ 1/k
Gibbs phenomenon: at jump discontinuities, partial sums always overshoot by ≈8.9% regardless of N.
Key Properties
Linearity: a·f+b·g ↔ a·F+b·G
Time shift: f(t−t₀) ↔ F(ω)·e^{−jωt₀} [magnitude unchanged, only phase shifts]
Freq shift: f(t)·e^{jω₀t} ↔ F(ω−ω₀) [AM modulation is multiplication by cos → shifts spectrum ±fc]
Convolution: f*h ↔ F·H [filtering = multiplication in frequency domain]
Parseval: ∫|f(t)|²dt = (1/2π)∫|F(ω)|²dω [energy is conserved between domains]

Re[F(ω)] and Im[F(ω)] — Complex spectrum components
F(ω) = Re[F] − j·Im[F]. Re part = ∫f(t)cos(ωt)dt (even symmetric). Im part = ∫f(t)sin(ωt)dt (odd symmetric).
