Maxwell's Equations — the foundation
∇·D = ρ_freeGauss's Law (electric) — enclosed free charge ∇·B = 0Gauss's Law (magnetic) — no magnetic monopoles ∇×E = −∂B/∂tFaraday's Law — changing B induces circulating E ∇×H = J + ∂D/∂tAmpere-Maxwell — currents + changing E induce H
Wave equation (free space, from Maxwell's curl equations):
∇²E = μ₀ε₀ ∂²E/∂t²   →   c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) ≈ 2.998×10⁸ m/s
Derivation: Apply ∇× to Faraday's Law → ∇×(∇×E) = −∂/∂t(∇×B). Use ∇×B = μ₀∂E/∂t and the vector identity ∇×(∇×E) = ∇(∇·E) − ∇²E. In free space ∇·E=0, giving ∇²E = μ₀ε₀∂²E/∂t². The FDTD simulators below discretize the curl equations directly on a staggered Yee grid.
